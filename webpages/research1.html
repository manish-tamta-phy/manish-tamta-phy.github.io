1) Thermal instability in an expanding box with cooling and heating effects

(Student: Manish Kumar Tamta, Research Supervisor: Dr. Prateek Sharma, 2022)

The gas surrounding galaxies outside their disks and inside virial radii is what we call a Circumgalactic medium (CGM). CGM is a multi-phase medium and a source of star-forming fuel. The physics of Gas inflow and outflow in the CGM can tell us how galaxy evolution depends on various physical factors.

There are many approaches for understanding our universe i.e. theory and observations.  The approach we use here is the hydrodynamic simulation in order to understand the physics of galaxy evolution.

The key idea is that thermal instability (TI) plays an important role in the dynamics of the gas and hence galaxy evolution. This is the reason we are interested in understanding the effects of TI in more depth.
