Pulsars:

Pulsars are fast rotating neutron stars which emit electromagnetic radiation from its magnetic poles. Usually the rotation axis of the pulsars is not along its magnetic poles hence the electromagnetic radiation sweeps the space like a light house.

Pulsars were first discovered by Jocelyn Bell Burnell using the radio telescopes at Cambridge University.
They are basically supernova remnents.
Pulsars (special type of neutron stars) are the extreme objects of our universe. The density is so high that one teaspoon of it would weigh about a mountain.
