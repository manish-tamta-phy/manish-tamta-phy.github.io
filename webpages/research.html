1) Thermal instability in an expanding box with cooling and heating effects

(Student: Manish Kumar Tamta, Research Supervisor: Dr. Prateek Sharma, 2022)

The gas surrounding galaxies outside their disks and inside virial radii is what we call a Circumgalactic medium (CGM). CGM is a multi-phase medium and a source of star-forming fuel. By Understanding the CGM we can understand the evolution of a galaxy. The physics of Gas inflow and outflow in the CGM can tell us how galaxies evolve.

There are many approaches to study our universe i.e. galaxies, plasma, and matter etc.  The approach we use is hydrodynamic simulation to understand the physics of galaxy evolution.

Now, thermal instability (TI) plays an important role in the dynamics of the gas and hence galaxy evolution. This is the reason we are interested in understanding the effects of TI in more depth.
